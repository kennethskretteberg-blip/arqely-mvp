## 2. Polygon-preview regression from conditional rAF (`7b7c61d`)

Reported: "walls do not show while drawing the room — they only appear after
setting the last corner."

Cause: the polygon branch of the canvas mousemove handler updated
`_polyMouseAngleDeg` but never called `render()`. Under the old unconditional
rAF loop this didn't matter — the next frame painted anyway. Under the new
conditional loop, no dirty flag meant no frame.

Fix: `_anyInteractionActive()` now also returns true when `S.ui.drawMode`,
`zoneDrawMode`, or `wallThicknessMode` is set. The canvas mousemove safety
net then issues `markDirty()` every frame in those modes — no need to touch
the individual drawMode branches.

## 3. Dimension-chain layout overhaul (`2c0cfdd`, `edd5098`, `8ead8a5`, `335dd30`, `0396e83`)

A multi-step iteration triggered by the report "room-dim and cable-dim
collide on the sides".

### Step 1 — `2c0cfdd`: add +40 px to room-dim when cable-dim is on

`_drawDimAnnotationsForRoom` baseOff was `78px / 48px` (with/without strip).
Cable-dim sits 38 px outside the wall — only 10 px gap on the side opposite
the cable's along-chain. `hitAnnotation` already accounted for `+40` when
cable-dim was on, but the actual drawing didn't — so visible labels collided
while click targets matched the *intended* position.

Added `+40px` to the room-dim baseOff and to `hitAnnotGizmo` for symmetry.

### Step 2 — `edd5098`: remove dashed extension lines from room-dim

The dashed lines from wall corners out to the dim chain crossed through the
cable-dim chain area, adding noise without aiding readability — the ticks
at the chain endpoints already imply the wall endpoints. Removed the
`moveTo(s1.x+nx*6, …)` block. Hindring annotations keep their ext-lines
(separate block lower in the same function).

### Step 3 — `8ead8a5`: convert all dim offsets to world-cm

User reported: "dim chains drift when I zoom — they should stay fixed."

Cause: offsets were hardcoded in screen pixels (48, 78, 38). At zoom-out the
chains crowd the room, at zoom-in they sit too close to the wall. Drift, in
practice.

Converted every dim-related offset to world-cm × `S.view.zoom * BASE_SCALE`:

- Room-dim baseline: **32 cm** (= 48 px @ zoom 1×, unchanged baseline)
- Strip-dim addon:   **+20 cm** (= 78 px total, unchanged)
- Cable-dim addon:   **+40 cm** (= 108 px total, was 88 px)
- Cable-/strip-dim chain BASE_OFF: **25 cm** (≈ 37.5 px, was 38 px)

Gap room-dim → cable-chain is now **47 cm world-cm** — constant across zoom.

Updated three call-sites in lockstep so render and hit-tests don't drift
apart: `_drawDimAnnotationsForRoom`, `hitAnnotation`, `hitAnnotGizmo`.
`_drawCableDimChainForRoomInner` and `_drawStripDimChainForRoom` BASE_OFF
also converted.

### Step 4 — `335dd30`: wall labels (V1-V4) always inside the room

User reported: "now sides are good, but the top is not."

Cause: the wall-name labels rendered at `(0, -4)` after `rotate(drawAng)`.
The rotation flips for "right-to-left" walls, but for a CW rect-polygon the
top wall (V1) is drawn left-to-right and ends up *outside* the room — right
in the path of the top room-dim chain. V2/V3/V4 happened to land inside.

Fix: compute the inside direction per wall using the room centroid, offset
the label 10 px inward, and use `textBaseline='middle'` with `(0, 0)`.
Symmetric for every wall regardless of polygon orientation.

### Step 5 — `0396e83`: bump cable-dim addon from +40 cm to +50 cm

After all the above, user reported the collision still appears in screenshots
of a 439×347 cm room. Increased the cable-dim addon to **+50 cm** so the
world-cm gap is **57 cm** (~85 px @ zoom 1×, ~114 px @ zoom 1.33). The user
may need a hard refresh to clear Vercel/browser cache.
